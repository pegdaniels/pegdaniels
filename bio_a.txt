<h1><p align="center"><strong><font color="#000099"><em>About Me</em></font></strong></p></h1>
<br />
<br />
<p align="justify"><span style="font-size : 1.2em;"><strong>Peg Daniels grew up in the Midwest, and she came to the South to obtain a Ph.D. in math. In addition to the degree, she acquired a math professor. Retiring early from her own career as a math professor at Auburn University in Auburn, Alabama, she&#8217;s turned to creative writing. Her published works in both fiction and creative nonfiction appear on these pages. A couple of short stories have won awards in writing competitions, and her soon&ndash;to&ndash;be&ndash;published story &#8220;Dixie Dawn&#8221; was a finalist in the prestigious <em>Black Warrior Review Fiction Contest</em>. The &#8220;Auburn version&#8221; of her short story, &#8220;My Sister, A Girl,&#8221; has been anthologized in <em>Chinaberries and Crows</em>.</p>

<p align="justify"><span style="font-size : 1.2em;"> Peg lives in Auburn with her one husband (the above-mentioned math professor) and two cats. She loves massage and meditation and swimming.</strong></span></p>
